Site Setup

Arrival flow · pull in to plugged in
The master checklist

Run this every time you arrive. Any step that leans on a system has a link to the detailed section — tap the note to jump.

Arrival Sequence
  1. 01 Pull in and park on the pad. Aim for approximately level to keep ram travel short.
  2. 02 Dump air. AIR DUMP wedged — nickel or sunglasses earpiece. Full procedure: Guide › Leveling.
  3. 03 Level the coach. RVA J-II panel — pads under the 3 rams, LEFT · FRONT · RIGHT to center the bubble. See Leveling.
  4. 04 Hook up 50 A shore power. Cord lives in bay D5. Pedestal breaker OFF while connecting, then ON.
  5. 05 Connect city water. City water inlet at the wet bay — D4.
  6. 06 Connect sewer. Wet bay D4. Keep the black and gray valves CLOSED until you're ready to dump.
  7. 07 Close blinds. Windshield privacy shade + front-cap shades; slide-out and bedroom last.
  8. 08 Verify shore power is working properly. Wait 30–45 s for the green EMS lights before switching on AC or any high-draw load. Rule: Electrical › No green, no AC.

Cook Camp

Outdoor kitchen · rugs, tables, grills, smoker · from bays D2 / D3 / P2
Where it lives, what to do

Everything for the outdoor kitchen lives in three bays. This is the layout first, then the setup order. Interior cupboards get their own section later.

Where It Lives
  • D3 Tables · benches · pop-up canopy (not the coach awning)
  • P2 Rugs · BBQ grill · Blackstone griddle + table · Green Mountain mini smoker · smoker pellets
  • D2 Grilling propane bottles · fire pit
Setup Order
  1. 01 Roll out the rugs and stake them down. Rugs from P2, rug stakes from D2. Everything else stages on top.
  2. 02 Raise the pop-up canopy over the P4 / P5 side for extra shade. Pop-up canopy from D3. This is the free-standing one — the coach's electric awning has its own procedure, see Awning.
  3. 03 Set out tables and benches under the canopy. From D3.
  4. 04 Assemble the Blackstone table + griddle in kitchen position. From P2.
  5. 05 Position the BBQ grill. From P2.
  6. 06 Set up the Green Mountain mini smoker; grab the pellet bag. Smoker + pellets from P2.
  7. 07 Set up the fire pit at a safe distance from the canopy. From D2.
  8. 08 Connect a grilling propane bottle to whichever unit you're using. Bottles live in D2. Snug hand-tight — no wrench on brass.
Teardown is this list in reverse

Propane off first, let grills cool, then break down in the reverse of setup. Rugs come up last so nothing gets stowed dirty.

Awning

Coach electric awning · key-enabled · rocker in entry stairwell
NEVER out in wind

Wind will tear the fabric and rip the arms off the coach. If wind picks up while it's extended, retract it immediately — even mid-meal. "No wind" means no wind now AND nothing coming in the forecast.

Sticky arms — plan for up to 3 people

The arms don't always slide clean. Expect them to catch. One person on the stairwell rocker; one or two more at the arms giving gentle shakes and light assist to unstick them. Never force it — that's how you bend an arm.

Two controls, not one

The awning has a key switch that enables power to the motor, and a separate rocker switch inside the entry stairwell that drives it in or out. Key must be ON before the rocker does anything. Key lives on the RV key ring, stored in the cupboard above the driver's seat.

Extend
  1. 01 Wind check. No wind now, nothing forecast. If either is uncertain, leave the awning in.
  2. 02 Ground clear under the awning path — furniture, string lights, kids.
  3. 03 Grab the RV key ring from the cupboard above the driver's seat. Insert the awning key and turn it ON. This enables power to the awning motor.
  4. 04 Entry stairwell — hold the rocker to OUT / EXTEND.
  5. 05 Watch both arms. If one catches, GENTLE shake and light assist to free it. Two more sets of hands ready. Never force.
  6. 06 Release the rocker at your desired extension. Turn the key OFF.
Retract
  1. 01 Clear everything under the awning — chairs, tables, cords, string lights.
  2. 02 Key ON.
  3. 03 Stairwell rocker — hold IN / RETRACT.
  4. 04 Assist the arms in as they travel. GENTLE shake if either sticks. Same rule: up to 3 people, never force.
  5. 05 Fully retracted — check both arms are seated flush against the coach.
  6. 06 Key OFF. Stow the key.
Rule of thumb

When in doubt, pull it in. A retracted awning has never been damaged by wind. Any doubt about forecast, sudden gust, or even just heading to bed — retract.

Leveling

RVA Model J-II · 3 hydraulic rams · under couch + D2 bay
Red light = jacks are down. Do not drive.

The red LED on the leveling panel means a jack is down while the engine is on. It must be OFF before moving the coach — driving on the jacks will wreck them.

Set Up & Level
  1. 01 Ignition key to ACC (accessory).
  2. 02 Turn the leveling system ON. Wait for the GREEN ready light.
  3. 03 Press AIR DUMP down and wedge it held. Use a nickel or a sunglasses earpiece to hold the rocker — it's momentary.
  4. 04 Let ALL the air out. The coach lowers all the way to the ground.
  5. 05 Slide a pad under each of the 3 jacks. Use the T-handle with the hook (same compartment) to drag the pads into position.
  6. 06 Extend / retract LEFT · FRONT · RIGHT to center the bubble level.
Panel Lights
  • GreenSystem on / ready to level.
  • RedJacks down — don't drive.
  • AmberWorking / not yet level.
Stow Before Driving
  1. 01 Key to ACC, leveling system ON.
  2. 02 RETRACT all three rams all the way up.
  3. 03 Pull the pads; stow pads and T-handle in the D2 bay.
  4. 04 Confirm the RED light is OFF.
  5. 05 Release AIR DUMP. Let the bags re-inflate — coach rises to ride height.
  6. 06 System OFF. Drive only with the red light out and coach fully aired up.

Electrical — Power Up

Intellitec 50 A Smart EMS · Trace RC-9 remote · Same cabinet as slide-out controls
Same cabinet, other switches

The panel that houses the Intellitec EMS and the Trace RC-9 also carries the slide-out room switch, systems heat, and storage lights — plus the "move driver's seat forward" and "disengage awning roller locks" warning stickers. Procedures for those go in their own sections when you send them.

Wait for the green lights first

When you connect shore power or start the generator, the electrical panel takes 30–45 seconds to come alive. Don't switch anything on during that delay — it protects the system.

Power Up
  1. 01 Connect shore power OR start the generator.
  2. 02 Wait 30–45 seconds. Don't touch any switches yet.
  3. 03 Watch the panel for the green lights = system settled and ready.
  4. 04 Only then turn on the roof AC or any high-draw item — microwave, water heater.
The rule

No green, no AC. Flipping a big load too early can trip the panel and you start over.

Generator

Cummins Onan Quiet Diesel 7500 · 7.5HDKAJ · 35 A 2-pole · 3 qt oil w/filter
Running erratic? Diesel wet stacking is the usual cause

On this Quiet Diesel 7500, "rough / hunting / erratic" almost always means wet stacking — unburned fuel and soot accumulating because the genset ran under-loaded for too long. Diesel engines need to work. The fix is to LOAD IT UP and run at 60%+ of rated output (~4.5–7 kW) for 1–2 hours to burn everything off. Full procedure below.

Cool it first — and this is diesel, no spark arrestor

Never wrench on a hot generator; muffler exceeds 400°F under load. 30-minute cool-down minimum. And forget the "3/4 bolt spark-arrestor cleanout" — that's a gas-Onan procedure. This HDKAJ diesel has no spark arrestor plug. Diesel maintenance is oil, filters, and load-recovery runs.

Model · hours · service parts

Cummins Onan 7.5HDKAJ · Quiet Diesel 7500 · 7.5 kW / 35 A 2-pole
Hours (last read): 2252.4
Air cleaner: 140-2897 · Oil filter: 185-5409 · Fuel filter: 149-2513
Oil: 3 qt w/filter, API CE. Viscosity 15W-40 (5–120°F), 10W-30 (-13–68°F), 5W-30 (-40–68°F).

Wet-Stacking Recovery Run
  1. 01 Confirm enough fuel for a 1–2 hour run under load. Rated 7.5 kW at full load burns roughly 0.7 gph diesel.
  2. 02 Turn the panel rotary switch to ON.
  3. 03 Press and HOLD Start / Preheat. Watch the red LED — that's the preheat glow-plug cycle. Hold 5–15 seconds; release when the engine catches.
  4. 04 Warm up 5–10 minutes at no load.
  5. 05 LOAD IT UP: both roof A/Cs on, water heater on electric, microwave running periodically. Target 4.5–7 kW draw (60–95% of rated). Under-loading is what caused the problem — solve it with load.
  6. 06 Run under load for 1–2 hours. Longer if it was severely wet-stacked. Listen: engine should settle from rough to smooth as heat builds. Watch exhaust: dark smoke should clear.
  7. 07 Cool-down: drop load to near zero for 2–3 min, then Stop.
  8. 08 Going forward: exercise the genset monthly at 50%+ load for 1–2 hours. Prevention beats cure.
Start / Preheat / Stop (from the coach panel)
  1. 01 Rotary switch to ON.
  2. 02 Press and HOLD Start / Preheat. Red LED on = preheat cycling. Diesel needs the glow plugs warm before firing. 5–15 seconds is typical, longer when cold.
  3. 03 Release when the engine catches. Let it settle 30–60 sec before adding load.
  4. 04 To stop: shed load, let it idle 30–60 sec (turbo cooldown discipline), then press Stop momentary.
  5. 05 Rotary switch to OFF when done.
Indicator Flash Codes
  • 1 rapid Preheat active
  • 2 rapid High engine temp — shed load, check coolant and airflow
  • 3 rapid Low oil pressure — STOP and check oil level immediately
  • Slow Service required — see service manual
Routine Maintenance (250 hr / annual)
  • Oil + filter3 qt, API CE, 15W-40. Filter 185-5409.
  • Fuel filterOnan 149-2513.
  • Air cleanerOnan 140-2897.
  • CoolantLevel check every start. Change per service manual interval.
  • ExhaustInspect manifold for leaks and wetness (wet = wet-stacking signal).
Prevention beats the cure

Run the genset monthly at 50%+ load for at least an hour. Wet stacking is caused by chronic under-loading; regular loaded exercise keeps combustion clean and the exhaust dry.

Flat-Tow

2017 Cadillac Escalade 4WD · 2-speed transfer case (N + 4LO) · Blue Ox tow bar · manual battery cutoff
Two things protect the truck

The transfer case MUST reach solid NEUTRAL (steps 4–5), and the steering must stay FREE — that only happens in ACC with the fob out. Never turn the ignition fully OFF while towing, or the wheel locks and grinds the front tires.

Hook Up & Go
  1. 01 Park level. Windows up, doors closed. Once power's cut you can't run the windows.
  2. 02 Set park brake. Start the engine.
  3. 03 Transmission to N (Neutral).
  4. 04 Transfer dial to 2HI, then turn toward N and HOLD 10+ seconds. N light blinks, then goes SOLID = transfer case in neutral.
  5. 05 Verify: shift to R, then D. Truck must NOT move. If it creeps, redo step 4.
  6. 06 Transmission to P (Park).
  7. 07 Engine OFF. Press START without the brake → ACC. Amber accessory light on. Take the fob with you.
  8. 08 Release the park brake.
  9. 09 Battery cutoff → OPEN. Confirm dead — dome light and horn off.
  10. 10 Connect tow bar, cables, lights, brake line — then run the Blue Ox check below.
The check that saves the transmission

R-then-D with zero movement (step 5). Every time.

Blue Ox Hookup Check
  1. Both arms fully extended and locked — pull coach forward a few inches, arms snap rigid.
  2. Every pivot and clevis pin has its clip or cotter pin — both ends.
  3. Safety cables crossed in an X, shackles threaded and pinned.
  4. Blue lighting cord plugged both ends — test brake, turn and markers.
  5. Breakaway cable clipped to the COACH, not the removable bar.
  6. Battery cutoff confirmed OPEN.
Unhook On Arrival
  1. 01 Disconnect tow bar, cables, lights, brake line.
  2. 02 Battery cutoff → CLOSED.
  3. 03 Set park brake, hold brake pedal, start engine.
  4. 04 Transmission to N.
  5. 05 Transfer dial to 4HI (or AUTO). N light goes off.
  6. 06 Shift to P. Confirm normal drive.
"Service 4WD" light after reconnect?

Known GM behavior from the power cycle (bulletin PIP5217). Usually clears on its own or after a short drive.

Plumbing — Shower Valve

Phoenix Faucets 4" two-handle · PF213350 · Lasco S-447-3NL stems
A leak new stems won't fix

On the Phoenix 4-inch valve, if fresh Lasco S-447-3NL stems don't stop the drip, it's almost always the cracked white plastic crossover / manifold tube inside the valve — a classic failure on these. Fix is a full valve replacement (Phoenix PF213350), not just stems.

Where the supply lines are

Red / blue PEX runs to the valve through the kitchen cabinet chase behind the galley — NOT a dedicated shower access panel (there isn't one in the factory manual).

Replace The Valve
  1. 01 Shut water off at the pump AND the city inlet. Open the shower to bleed pressure.
  2. 02 Open the galley cabinet chase to reach the red / blue PEX runs. No dedicated shower panel — go in through the kitchen.
  3. 03 Pull trim, remove handles and escutcheons; unscrew and lift out the old Phoenix valve body.
  4. 04 Fit the new Phoenix PF213350 with SharkBite / PEX female adapters. PTFE tape on threads. Hand-tight, then a quarter-turn — do NOT overtorque the plastic fittings.
  5. 05 Charge the system slowly. Check both stems and every joint for weep. Cycle hot / cold a few times.
  6. 06 Re-fit handles, escutcheons, trim. Confirm no drip after a 15-minute soak test.
